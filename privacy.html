Privacy Policy

N-Back · Effective July 18, 2026

N-Back does not collect, transmit, or share any data. Everything you do in the app stays on your device.

What we collect

Nothing. N-Back has no user accounts, no third-party analytics or advertising SDKs, and no tracking. There is no server behind the app — we never see your data. The app's only network communication is with Apple's App Store, to show current prices and process optional purchases (see "Purchases" below); that traffic goes to Apple, not to us.

The only usage information we can ever see comes from Apple, not from the app: if you have opted in to sharing analytics with app developers (iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Analytics & Improvements), Apple provides us with aggregated download, usage, and crash statistics that do not identify you. That sharing is optional, controlled entirely by you in iOS, and covered by Apple's privacy policy.

Data stored on your device

To work, N-Back stores the following locally on your device only:

  • Game settings — N-level, stimulus types, timing, and other preferences.
  • Game history and statistics — the results of your training sessions, used for the in-app charts, streaks, and level suggestions.

Like any app's local data, this may be included in your device backups (iCloud or computer backups) if you have them enabled — that is controlled by you through iOS, not by us.

Notifications

If you enable the optional daily training reminder, N-Back schedules a local notification on your device. No server is involved. You can revoke notification permission at any time in iOS Settings.

Purchases

If you make an optional purchase in the app (such as N-Back Pro), the transaction is processed entirely by Apple through your Apple Account. We do not receive your name, email address, or payment details, and purchase entitlements are verified on your device. See Apple's privacy policy for how Apple handles purchase data.

Deleting your data

  • In the app: Settings → Clear All Statistics removes your game history.
  • Deleting the app removes all of its data from your device.

Because we receive no data from the app, there is nothing we could access, export, or delete on your behalf — deletion is entirely in your control, on your device.

Children

N-Back collects no data from anyone, including children.

Changes to this policy

If the app's data practices ever change, this page will be updated with a new effective date before the change takes effect.

That can include processing on servers outside the European Economic Area; such transfers are covered by safeguards Google provides, including its certification under the EU–U.S. Data Privacy Framework and EU standard contractual clauses. Our legal basis for this under the GDPR is our legitimate interest in responding to you (Article 6(1)(f) GDPR).

You can ask us at any time to access, correct, or delete that correspondence, or to object to or restrict our processing of it, and you have the right to lodge a complaint with a data protection authority — in Poland, the President of the Personal Data Protection Office (UODO).
